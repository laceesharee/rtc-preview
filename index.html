Service Description

Round The Clock Notary provides mobile notary and remote online notarization services in accordance with Texas law. The notary public serves as an impartial witness to the signing of documents and verifies the identity of each signer.

The notary does not represent any party in the transaction and does not participate in the legal content of documents.

The State of Texas sets the maximum fee for each notarial act. Travel, scheduling, printing, and convenience services are separate service fees charged in addition to the state-regulated notarial fee.

The notary public is not an attorney and does not provide legal advice. The notary will not interpret legal documents, recommend which documents to use, or advise how documents should be completed. If legal advice is required, consult a licensed attorney before scheduling service.

Client Responsibility for Documents

Clients are responsible for ensuring all documents are complete and ready for signature before the appointment. The notary does not prepare legal documents and does not determine which notarial act is required. Documents containing blank spaces or incomplete sections cannot be notarized under Texas law. If documents are incomplete or otherwise improper under Texas notary law, the notary must refuse service. Travel fees still apply in this circumstance.

Identification Requirements

All signers must present valid government-issued photo identification at the time of the appointment. Acceptable identification includes a current driver's license, state identification card, passport, or other identification permitted under Texas law. The name on the identification must reasonably match the name on the document being notarized.

Language Requirement

Notary services are currently provided only in English. The notary must communicate directly with each signer to confirm identity, willingness, and awareness of the document being signed. If direct communication cannot be established, service will be refused.

Deposit and Appointment Policy

A deposit is required to secure all appointments and is applied toward the final service total. The deposit becomes non-refundable under the following conditions:

  • Appointment cannot be completed due to missing identification
  • Appointment cannot be completed due to incomplete or improper documents
  • Signer refuses to proceed after the notary arrives

If an appointment must be rescheduled due to incomplete documents or missing identification, a new appointment and new deposit are required.

Travel Fees

Mobile service includes travel time and distance from the notary's point of origin to the appointment location. Travel fees apply once the notary begins travel to the appointment location. Travel fees are non-refundable and still apply in full if the notarization cannot be completed due to missing identification, incomplete documents, signer absence, or refusal to proceed.

Wait Time Policy

The 15-minute waiting period begins at the scheduled appointment time, not the notary's arrival time. If the signer has not appeared and the notarization has not commenced by the end of the 15-minute window, the visit will be treated as a completed service call and all applicable fees and deposits are forfeited.

Full payment is due at the time of service.

Right to Refuse Service

Texas law requires a notary public to refuse a notarization if: proper identification is not presented; the signer appears unwilling or unaware; the document is incomplete; the requested notarial act violates Texas law; or fraud, coercion, or misrepresentation is suspected. If service is refused for any of these reasons, the appointment will be treated as a completed service visit and the deposit will not be refunded.

Remote Online Notarization

Online notarizations follow Texas Remote Online Notary requirements. Identity verification, credential analysis, and audio-video recording are required by law. Additional platform fees may apply and are the responsibility of the client.

Recordkeeping

Texas law requires the notary to maintain a record of each notarization. Information recorded may include the date, type of notarization, document description, identification type, and location of the notarization.
